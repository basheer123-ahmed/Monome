Foundation

Choosing the Right Foundation Type for Your Plot

The foundation is the most critical element of any building. Choosing the wrong foundation type can lead to differential settlement, wall cracks, and in severe cases, structural failure. The right choice depends on your soil's bearing capacity (SBC), which is determined by geotechnical testing.

Isolated Footings: Best for plots with SBC above 15 T/m². Used when columns are spaced far apart and loads are moderate. Most economical for G+1 and G+2 residential buildings on firm soil.

Raft Foundation: Used when SBC is low (below 10 T/m²) or when columns are closely spaced. The entire base of the building is a single continuous slab. Provides excellent load distribution and is ideal for apartments and multi-floor structures.

Pile Foundation: Required for very soft soils, marshy land, or high-rise buildings. Steel or concrete piles are driven deep into the ground until they reach a hard stratum. More expensive but essential for challenging soil conditions.

At MONOME, we always conduct soil testing before finalizing the foundation type — never skipping this critical step regardless of the project budget.

Waterproofing

The Complete Guide to Waterproofing Your Home

Water ingress is the number one cause of structural damage in Indian homes — ahead of poor concrete quality and substandard steel. A comprehensive waterproofing strategy protects your investment for decades.

Cementitious Waterproofing: A rigid system applied as a slurry coat to basements, bathrooms, and sumps. Easy to apply and bond well to concrete, but cracks when the structure moves. Best for areas with minimal structural movement.

Polyurethane (PU) Membrane: A flexible, seamless membrane applied to terraces and external balconies. Accommodates thermal expansion and contraction. Brands like Dr. Fixit and Fosroc PU systems offer 10-year guarantees. Our preferred choice for terraces.

Crystalline Waterproofing: Penetrates concrete capillaries and crystallizes to block water permanently. Ideal for basements and underground structures where external access for repairs is impossible.

MONOME applies a multi-layer waterproofing system on all critical zones — we never rely on a single coat or single system.

Electrical

Home Electrical Planning: What Every Owner Must Know

Poor electrical planning causes up to 40% of residential fire incidents in India. Planning your electrical system correctly from the start saves cost, ensures safety, and future-proofs your home.

Load Calculation: Every home needs a proper load calculation (total watts of all appliances + 20% safety factor). This determines the main circuit breaker size and the number of sub-circuits required. A 2,000 sqft home typically needs a 60-100A main switch.

Circuit Zoning: Separate circuits for AC units, kitchen appliances, and lighting reduce load on any single circuit and make fault isolation easier. MONOME always provides dedicated circuits for each AC, kitchen, and heavy appliance.

Earthing System: A proper 3-pin earthing system with periodic testing is critical. We use copper earth wires and conduct an earth resistance test to verify below 5Ω before handover.

EV Charging Readiness: We recommend installing a 32A dedicated circuit and conduit from the basement or car porch to the meter room during construction, at minimal incremental cost.

Materials

AAC vs Red Brick vs Fly-Ash Brick: Which to Choose?

Red Clay Brick: Traditional fired brick. Good compressive strength (~3.5 MPa) but high water absorption (~20%) makes it vulnerable to moisture ingress. High carbon footprint due to kiln firing. Weight: ~3 kg/unit.

Fly-Ash Brick: Made from thermal power plant waste + lime + gypsum. Eco-friendly (recycled waste), lower water absorption (~8-12%), good strength (4-5 MPa), and precise dimensions reducing mortar wastage. Cost-effective and MONOME's standard choice.

AAC Block (Autoclaved Aerated Concrete): Lightweight (only 600 kg/m³ vs 1800 kg/m³ for brick), excellent thermal insulation, and soundproofing. Reduces dead load on structure, potentially saving 10-15% on structural steel. Higher material cost but reduces plastering work significantly.

MONOME uses fly-ash bricks as standard and recommends AAC blocks for premium projects or top-floor additions where weight reduction matters.

Windows

UPVC vs Aluminium Windows: A Buyer's Guide

UPVC Windows: Unplasticised PVC with multi-chambered profiles. Excellent thermal and acoustic insulation, no corrosion, no painting required, and 30+ year lifespan. Ideal for bedrooms and living rooms where quietness and energy efficiency matter. Brands: Fenesta, Rehau, Veka.

Aluminium Windows: Slim profiles with large glass spans, modern aesthetics, and slimmer frame sections. Lower insulation than UPVC but handles larger openings (2m+ spans) better. Powder-coated finish is durable but can oxidize in coastal areas. Better for large living room sliding doors.

Double Glazing: Both materials benefit from double-glazed units (6mm-12mm air gap-6mm). Reduces heat gain by 40-50% and noise by 30dB. Recommended for bedrooms facing roads or in hot climates. MONOME makes double glazing standard in premium and luxury packages.

Our recommendation: UPVC for bedrooms and bathrooms; aluminium for large living room openings and facade statements.

Sustainability

Building Green: How to Make Your Home Energy Efficient

A green home is not just eco-friendly — it's cost-effective long-term. Energy-efficient homes save 30-50% on electricity and water bills annually. Here are the most impactful green strategies MONOME incorporates:

Solar Pre-Wiring: We install conduit and dedicated wiring from the rooftop to the electrical panel during construction. Adding solar panels later becomes a plug-and-play exercise, saving ₹15,000-25,000 in retrofitting costs.

Rainwater Harvesting: A 10,000-liter underground sump connected to roof drainage reduces municipal water dependency by 30-40%. BBMP-mandated in Bangalore for plots above 2,400 sqft.

Passive Cooling: Oriented windows for cross-ventilation, terrace gardens, and light-colored roof tiles can reduce indoor temperatures by 4-6°C, lowering AC load significantly.

Low-VOC Paints: Asian Paints Royale Aspira and similar low-VOC emulsions reduce indoor air pollution by 80% compared to standard paints. MONOME uses these as standard across all interior surfaces.

GLOSSARY

Construction Terminology Center

Decode construction jargon. Your A-to-Z guide to understanding every term your contractor uses.

🔍

RCC

Reinforced Cement Concrete

Concrete embedded with steel bars (rebar) to resist tensile forces. RCC columns, beams, and slabs form the structural skeleton of a building.

TMT Steel

Thermo-Mechanically Treated Bars

High-strength steel bars used as reinforcement in RCC. Fe-500D and Fe-550D are the most common grades, with 'D' denoting improved ductility for earthquake resistance.

BUA

Built-Up Area

The total floor area including carpet area, wall thickness, and balcony area. Construction rates are typically quoted on BUA.

Plinth Level

Raised Ground Floor Base Height

The height at which the ground floor is raised from natural ground level (typically 300-600mm). Prevents flooding and groundwater ingress.

SBC

Soil Bearing Capacity

The maximum load per unit area that soil can support without shear failure. Determined by geotechnical testing. Critical for foundation design.

BOQ

Bill of Quantities

A detailed itemized list of materials, quantities, and unit costs for a construction project. Used for cost estimation and contract pricing.

MEP

Mechanical, Electrical & Plumbing

The three major building services systems — HVAC, electrical wiring, and plumbing — that make a building functional. Typically 20-25% of construction cost.

PCC

Plain Cement Concrete

Concrete without steel reinforcement. Used as a leveling bed below foundations and floors. Typical mix: M10 or M15 grade.

DPC

Damp Proof Course

A horizontal layer of waterproof material (bituminous felt or rich cement mortar) laid at plinth level to prevent rising dampness from ground to walls.

FAR / FSI

Floor Area Ratio / Space Index

The ratio of total built-up area to plot area. A FAR of 2 on a 1,200 sqft plot allows 2,400 sqft total construction. Determined by local municipal regulations.

OPC / PPC

Ordinary / Portland Pozzolana Cement

OPC 53 grade is used for structural concrete. PPC (with fly-ash additive) is used for plastering and masonry. Both are IS-certified for quality.

Curing

Concrete Hydration Process

The process of maintaining moisture and temperature in freshly poured concrete for 28 days to achieve full design strength. Skipping curing can reduce concrete strength by 40%.
